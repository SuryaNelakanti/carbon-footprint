Our
World
Is Burning.

In 2018, our carbon emission was upwards of 100 MILLION tons of Carbon Dioxide.

Our emissions are on track to increase heavily in 2021, with 2020 showing our first dip in emissions in a long time.
(Due to the covid 19 pandemic).


To see how we have come to this, we have to look at our carbon impact throughout the ages. So, let us take a step back.

A big step
back

Pre Industrial Age
14000 BC onwards


  • Our ancestors start to transition from hunter-gatherers to farmers.

  • To grow crops we start to clear forests.

  • Now, we clear forests equal to 30,000km2 per year.

Industrial Age
1750 AD onwards


  • We enter the industrialized age, a mechanized age, powered by coal.

  • From this time human activities and pollution are closely interlinked.

1781


  • Discovery of the steam engine, which paves the way into the industrial revolution, allowing railway locomotives to operate.

  • CO2 emissions start to rise. Human impact of CO2 emissions are 1 GtCO2.(One billion tonnes of carbon dioxide)

1850


  • Marks the beginning of using oil as a fuel and a raw material for many industrial processes.

  • By the end of the century it accounts for one-third of the global carbon emissions.

  • Human impact of CO2 emissions are 120 GtCO2.(One twenty billion tonnes of carbon dioxide)

1880


  • Construction of first high pressure gas cylinder, which leads to industrial exploitation of natural gases.

  • Right now, gas accounts for one-third of global carbon emissions.

  • Human impact of CO2 emissions are 220 GtCO2.(Two twenty billion tonnes of carbon dioxide)

1908


  • T Ford is released, the first mass produced car. All of a sudden, automobiles are accesible to the common public.

  • Right now, more than one billion people own a car, road transport generating 17% of the worlds CO2 emissions.

  • Human inpact of CO2 emissions are 355 GtCO2.(Three hundred and fifty five billion tonnes of carbon dioxide)

1950


  • Civil aviation undergoes a boom, with flights becoming cheap enough for the common man to use.

  • Right now, aviation accounts for 3% of the worlds CO2 emissions.

  • Human impact of CO2 emissions are 685 GtCO2.(Six hundred and eighty five billion tonnes of carbon dioxide)

Information Age
1970s onwards


  • Motor vehicle transport grows uncontollably.

  • Emissions from this sector are growing faster than any other energy sector and it relies almost entirely on petroleum supplies.

  • Human impact of CO2 emissions are 990 GtCO2.(Nine hundred and ninety billion tonnes of carbon dioxide)
